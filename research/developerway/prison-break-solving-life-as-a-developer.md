# Prison break, or solving life like a developer

**Published:** 15-11-2021
**Author:** Nadia Makarevich

## The origin story

The author had long contemplated relocating to Europe for a year or two. After spending four years in Australia, she felt increasingly restless. When COVID-19 hit and Australia entered prolonged lockdowns in mid-2021, she decided the time had come to act. Despite facing seemingly insurmountable obstacles—a closed border, no legal right to work in Europe, and an existing job in Sydney—she approached the challenge methodically.

## The Relocation Project

Rather than dwelling on the massive goal, she broke it into smaller, manageable pieces:

**The project:** Relocate to Europe for one year

**MVP (Minimal Viable Product):**
- Legal right to live and work in Europe for a year
- A reliable source of income for a year
- A way to leave Australia

### A reliable source of income for a year

She worked at a startup with founders who believed in supporting employees. When she discussed the possibility of remote work from Europe with her boss, he immediately agreed without hesitation.

### Legal right to live and work in Europe for a year

Through research, she discovered digital nomad visas offered by several countries. Malta's Nomad Residence Permit stood out because it provided:

- One-year residency with extension options
- English-speaking population
- Access to the Schengen zone (25 countries)
- No double-income taxation
- Mediterranean culture and coastline

The application process was straightforward: she submitted required documents, paid fees, and received approval within three weeks.

### A way to leave Australia

This proved the most challenging component. Australia had closed its borders in March 2020 and maintained restrictions through November 2021. To leave, citizens needed:

- A very valid reason
- Documentary proof of that reason
- Department of Immigration exemption approval

Around half of applications were denied. Common rejections included visiting dying parents, job offers abroad, family reunification, studying abroad, or permanent relocation.

She created a mini-project within the larger project:

**Step 1:** Research successful applications and extract common patterns
**Step 2:** Collect necessary documents
**Step 3:** Submit Statutory Declaration—a legal document stating:

- Intent to leave for at least one year
- Evidence of serious commitment (selling car, ending lease, obtaining visa)
- Financial capacity (bank statements, employment letters)
- Understanding of risks and non-reliance on government assistance

The declaration carried consequences: returning within three months would constitute criminal offense with significant fines or imprisonment.

Her application was approved.

## The new beginning

Once the MVP was complete, remaining tasks became manageable. She got vaccinated, sold her car, ended her lease, disposed of possessions, and booked her flight.

She now resides in Malta, surrounded by cats and planning future adventures. "May the cats be with you."
